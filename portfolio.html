Selective Experience Replay for Lifelong Learning in Trading

Research Project · Reinforcement Learning

We explore whether employing a long-term memory buffer in stock trading tasks helps agents learn better strategies. Our approach involves implementing Selective Experience Replay, using four prioritization strategies: favoring surprise, favoring reward, matching the global training distribution, and maximizing coverage of the state space. Furthermore, we explore a new prioritization strategy using change-point detection on financial time series.

Tech: PyTorch, StableBaselines3, FinRL, Git

Studying the extension of ordinary logic with natural numbers and recursion over natural numbers

Research Project · Logic and Computation

Gödel's System T is an extension of the Natural Deduction Calculus (ND) which introduces the natural numbers as a connective. System T also introduces a recursion scheme called Primitive Recursion over the natural numbers. Our project focused on the proof of Weak Normalization and mechanization of System T in Beluga, a proof assistant.

Tech: Beluga, Git
